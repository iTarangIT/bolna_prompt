# Cold-Call Sales Methodology for Voice AI Agents

Best practices for B2B outbound cold-calling, adapted for AI voice agents selling industrial products to dealers.

---

## 1. Cold-Call Opening Frameworks

The first 15 seconds determine whether the dealer stays on the line. Three proven patterns:

### Permission-Based Opening (recommended for cold leads)
Introduce yourself, state the purpose in one line, and ask permission to continue. This respects the dealer's time and reduces immediate hang-ups.

Pattern: Name + Company + One-line value + Permission ask

Example flow: "Hi, I'm [name] from [company]. We make [product] that [single benefit]. Do you have a quick minute?"

Why it works: Dealers who say "yes" to the permission question have psychologically committed to listening. Those who say "no" give you a clean exit to reschedule instead of a hostile hang-up.

### Value-First Opening (recommended for warm/hot leads)
Lead with the benefit before asking anything. Skip permission if the lead has shown prior interest.

Pattern: Name + Company + Specific value proposition + Soft transition to discovery

### Pattern Interrupt (use sparingly, for re-engagement)
Break the expected cold-call script with something unexpected — a specific reference to their business, a surprising fact, or a direct acknowledgment that this is a sales call.

Pattern: "I know you probably get a lot of sales calls — I'll keep this to two minutes."

## 2. Qualification Framework (BANT for Dealers)

Qualify leads early to avoid wasting time on non-buyers. Adapt BANT for dealer B2B:

### Budget
- Does the dealer buy batteries regularly? What volume?
- Are they price-sensitive or value-sensitive?
- Detection: Ask about current monthly volume, not "what's your budget?"

### Authority
- Is this person the decision-maker or a gatekeeper?
- In Indian dealer businesses, the owner usually decides. If speaking to staff, politely ask to speak with the owner.
- Detection: "Are you the one who handles battery purchases?"

### Need
- Does the dealer actually need what you sell? Are they in the right segment?
- Detection: Ask about their current battery setup, what they sell/service.

### Timeline
- Is there urgency? Are they running low on stock? Expanding?
- Detection: "Are you looking to place an order soon, or just exploring options?"

**Qualification shortcut:** If lead data is pre-injected (name, shop, location, interest level), you already know most of this. Never re-ask what you already know — it destroys trust.

## 3. Objection Handling: AIRC Framework

When a dealer objects, follow this four-step sequence:

### A — Acknowledge
Show you heard and respect their concern. Never dismiss or argue immediately.
- "बिल्कुल, I completely understand."
- "That's a fair point, sir."
- "हाँ, many dealers have the same concern initially."

### I — Isolate
Confirm this is the real objection, not a polite brush-off.
- "Is price the main thing holding you back, or is there something else too?"
- "Other than [objection], is there anything else you'd want to know?"

### R — Respond
Address the specific objection with evidence, not generic reassurance.
- Price → Total cost of ownership, bulk pricing, longer lifecycle
- Quality → OEM certification, warranty terms, specific test data
- Service → Nearest service center, response time commitment
- Existing supplier → Position as complementary, not replacement; offer trial order

### C — Confirm
Check that your response actually resolved the concern before moving forward.
- "Does that make sense?"
- "Would that address your concern?"
- "क्या यह बात clear है?"

**Critical rule:** Never skip Acknowledge. Jumping straight to a counter-argument makes the dealer feel unheard and triggers defensiveness.

## 4. Closing Techniques for Phone Sales

### Trial Close (test readiness throughout)
Drop small closes throughout the conversation to gauge readiness:
- "If the pricing works out, would you be open to a trial order?"
- "Would you like me to have our representative visit with samples?"

### Alternative Close (give two yes-options)
Instead of yes/no, offer a choice between two positive outcomes:
- "Would you prefer delivery this week or next?"
- "Should I send the 48V details or the 60V specs?"

### Next-Step Close (always advance, never leave hanging)
Every call must end with a concrete next step, even if it's not a sale:
- Interested → Schedule visit or send catalogue
- Uncertain → Schedule follow-up call at specific time
- Not interested → Plant a seed for future: "If things change, we're here"

### Assumptive Close (for hot leads showing buying signals)
When the dealer is asking specific questions about delivery, pricing, or specs, assume the sale is happening:
- "Great, I'll get the pricing for 50 units sent to you on WhatsApp."
- "Let me check delivery to [location] — should I book a visit too?"

## 5. Rejection Recovery

### Soft No ("not right now", "call later", "send on WhatsApp")
These are deferrals, not rejections. Respond with:
1. Accept gracefully — "बिल्कुल, no problem at all."
2. Get a micro-commitment — "When would be a good time to call back?" or "I'll send catalogue on WhatsApp, shall I follow up Thursday?"
3. Plant a seed — leave one memorable fact they'll recall next time.

### Hard No ("not interested", "don't need", "stop calling")
Respect it immediately:
1. Thank them sincerely — "Thank you for your time, sir."
2. Leave the door open — "If you ever need lithium batteries, iTarang is here."
3. Exit cleanly — no guilt-tripping, no "but sir just one minute."

### "Who are you?" / Confusion
The dealer doesn't know who called. This means the opening failed:
1. Re-introduce clearly — name, company, one-line purpose.
2. Apologize for the confusion: "Sorry sir, let me introduce properly."
3. Do not repeat the exact same opening — rephrase it shorter and clearer.

## 6. Conversation Metrics & Benchmarks

### Talk Ratio
- Target: 40% agent, 60% dealer (in discovery phase)
- If agent is talking more than 60%, they're pitching too early
- In product presentation, 60% agent / 40% dealer is acceptable

### Questions Per Call
- Minimum 2-3 discovery questions before any product pitch
- Never ask more than 2 questions in a row without giving value back
- After every question answered, acknowledge before the next question

### Call Duration
- Cold call target: 2-4 minutes (anything longer and you're losing them)
- Warm call: 3-6 minutes
- If call exceeds target without advancing, offer to schedule a follow-up

### Advancement Rate
- Every call should produce a next step (visit, callback, WhatsApp, or clean close)
- "I'll call back sometime" is not a next step — get a specific day/time
- Sending a catalogue without a follow-up date is a dead end
