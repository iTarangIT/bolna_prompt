# Dealer Psychology & Objection Map

Understanding how e-rickshaw battery dealers think, what drives their decisions, and how to handle specific objections. Focused on the Indian lithium-ion battery market.

---

## 1. Dealer Decision Hierarchy

When a dealer evaluates a new battery supplier, their concerns stack in this order (highest fear first):

### 1. Service & Support Fear (strongest barrier)
"If the battery fails, will someone come fix it? How fast?"
- Dealers have been burned by suppliers who vanish after the sale
- Service availability matters more than product quality in their mind
- A dealer would rather buy an inferior battery with guaranteed service than a superior one with uncertain support

### 2. Price Sensitivity
"Is this going to cost me more than what I currently pay?"
- Dealers think in per-unit cost, not total cost of ownership
- They compare against their current supplier's price, not market rate
- Bulk pricing and credit terms are often more important than unit price

### 3. Quality and Reliability Doubt
"Will these batteries actually last as long as you claim?"
- Claims of longer life, higher efficiency sound like every other salesperson
- Dealers trust their own experience and other dealers' reviews over spec sheets
- OEM certification (e.g., Trontek) is a strong credibility signal

### 4. Inertia / Switching Cost
"My current supplier works fine. Why change?"
- Even if the current supplier isn't great, the known devil is preferred
- Switching requires learning new products, explaining to customers, adjusting stock
- Relationship with current supplier may include credit terms, personal rapport

---

## 2. Supplier-Switching Barriers

Understanding why dealers resist switching — even when presented with a better product:

### Existing Credit Terms
Many dealers buy on credit from their current supplier (30-60 day payment cycles). Switching to a new supplier often means paying upfront until trust is established. This is a real financial barrier.

**Counter:** Offer to match or introduce flexible payment terms. Even "pay after delivery" for the first order reduces risk.

### Relationship Lock-In
Indian B2B relationships are personal. The dealer's current supplier rep might be someone they've known for years. Switching feels like betrayal.

**Counter:** Position as "adding a supplier" not "replacing." "Sir, हम आपके existing supplier की जगह नहीं लेना चाहते — बस एक option और add करना चाहते हैं।"

### Customer Familiarity
The dealer's customers know the current battery brand. Introducing a new brand means the dealer has to sell twice — first to themselves, then to their customers.

**Counter:** Emphasize OEM partnership ("Same Trontek cells, अलग packaging") or offer demo units so the dealer's customers can try before the dealer commits.

### Fear of Unknown Service
"What if I need warranty service and you don't pick up the phone?"

**Counter:** Offer concrete proof: nearest service center location, representative visit, WhatsApp support line, response time commitment.

## 3. OEM Credibility Building

For lithium-ion batteries, the OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) relationship is the strongest credibility signal.

### Why OEM Matters to Dealers
- Dealers know that battery quality comes from the cell manufacturer, not the assembler
- Trontek, Coslight, EVE, CATL — these names carry weight
- "OEM certified" means the cells are genuine, not second-grade or recycled

### How to Frame OEM Partnership
- Lead with the OEM name: "Trontek certified cells"
- Explain what it means: "Same cells that [known brand] uses"
- Differentiate from assemblers: "We don't just assemble — Trontek manufactures specifically for our specs"

### What NOT to Claim
- Don't claim to be the OEM if you're a reseller/assembler
- Don't name-drop OEMs without a real relationship
- Don't compare OEM quality against unnamed competitors ("unlike others" — dealers see through this)

## 4. Technical Anxiety Management

Many dealers, especially smaller ones, don't deeply understand lithium vs lead-acid technology. They've heard claims from multiple salespeople and are skeptical.

### Simplification Patterns

**Voltage:** "48 Volt battery — आपकी standard e-rickshaw ke liye perfect fit"
- Don't explain voltage theory. Just confirm compatibility.

**Capacity (Ah):** "100 Ampere Hour — means your e-rickshaw runs longer per charge"
- Translate Ah into practical benefit (range, runtime)

**Lifecycle:** "2000 cycles — roughly five to six years of daily use"
- Always convert technical specs to time-based benefit

**Lithium vs Lead-Acid:** "Half the weight, double the life, no water filling needed"
- Three concrete differences, not a chemistry lecture
- Acknowledge lead-acid familiarity: "Lead-acid is reliable — lithium is just the next step"

### When Dealer Asks Technical Questions
- If the question is within your knowledge: answer simply and directly
- If the question is complex: "Sir, यह technical detail है — मैं अपनी engineering team से confirm करके बताता हूँ। WhatsApp पर भेज दूँ?"
- Never bluff technical specs. Getting caught in a wrong answer destroys all credibility.

## 5. Objection-Response Map

### "Already have a supplier" (most common)
- Acknowledge: "बिल्कुल sir, most good dealers already have established suppliers."
- Reframe: "We're not asking you to switch — बस एक additional option consider करें।"
- Value hook: "Many dealers keep two suppliers — better pricing leverage and no stock-outs."
- Next step: "Can I send you our catalogue? Just for comparison — no commitment."

### "Too expensive" / "Price ज़्यादा है"
- Acknowledge: "हाँ, upfront cost definitely higher है than lead-acid."
- Reframe to TCO: "Per cycle cost actually कम आती है — 2000 cycles vs 300. Overall savings in five years."
- Offer tier: "हमारे पास different capacity options हैं — let me find what fits your budget."
- Bulk option: "10 units से ऊपर पर special pricing available है।"

### "Bad experience with lithium" / "पहले try किया, अच्छा नहीं रहा"
- Acknowledge: "That's unfortunate, sir. I've heard this from other dealers too."
- Isolate: "क्या battery life issue था, या service?"
- Differentiate: "हमारी batteries Trontek OEM cells use करती हैं — [specific differentiator]."
- Risk reducer: "Warranty के साथ — अगर कोई issue आता है, हमारी service team handle करेगी।"

### "Not interested" / "नहीं चाहिए"
- Accept immediately: "बिल्कुल sir, कोई बात नहीं।"
- Plant seed: "बस एक बात — अगर future में lithium explore करना हो, iTarang याद रखिए।"
- Exit cleanly. Don't argue, don't guilt-trip, don't ask why.

### "Send on WhatsApp" / "WhatsApp पर भेजो"
- This can be genuine interest OR a brush-off. Qualify:
- If during active conversation: genuine. "बिल्कुल, catalogue भेज देता हूँ। किस voltage range में interested हैं?"
- If said to end the call: brush-off. Send catalogue but schedule a follow-up: "भेज देता हूँ — Thursday को follow up करूँ?"

### "Call later" / "बाद में call करो" / "Busy हूँ"
- Accept: "बिल्कुल sir, कोई problem नहीं।"
- Get specific: "कब convenient होगा — कल morning या afternoon?"
- If they refuse to give a time: "ठीक है sir, मैं day after tomorrow try करता हूँ। धन्यवाद।"

### "Who are you?" / "कौन बोल रहे हो?"
- This means the opening failed. Re-introduce clearly:
- "Sir, मैं Vikram, iTarang Technologies से बोल रहा हूँ। हम lithium-ion batteries बनाते हैं e-rickshaws के लिए।"
- Keep it to one sentence. Don't repeat the full opening script.

### "Your product is the same as everyone else"
- Don't argue sameness. Shift to service: "Product तो similar दिख सकता है — difference हमारी service और warranty में है।"
- Offer proof: "Sir, एक visit schedule करते हैं — हमारा representative samples लेकर आएगा, आप खुद compare कर लीजिए।"

## 6. Buying Signals to Detect

When a dealer shows these behaviors, they're interested — shift from discovery to closing:

### Strong Buying Signals
- Asking about specific voltage/capacity: "64 Volt में क्या options हैं?"
- Asking about delivery: "Delivery कितने दिन में होगी?"
- Asking about pricing for specific quantities: "50 units पर क्या rate होगा?"
- Mentioning competitor by name for comparison: "Trontek से direct लें तो?"
- Asking about payment terms: "Credit available है?"

### Moderate Buying Signals
- Asking for catalogue or WhatsApp details (during engaged conversation)
- Asking about warranty specifics ("Warranty claim process क्या है?")
- Mentioning their customer demand ("हमारे customers lithium माँगते हैं")

### False Signals (not buying, just polite)
- "Interesting" without follow-up questions
- "Send on WhatsApp" as conversation ender
- "I'll think about it" without asking for specifics

---

## 7. Trust Signals That Matter to Indian Dealers

In order of impact:

1. **Personal visit offer** — "हमारा representative आपकी shop पर आएगा" (shows you're real, invested)
2. **Local service center** — "आपके area में service center है" (reduces service fear)
3. **Other dealers in their area** — "आपके पास [city] में कई dealers हैं हमारे" (social proof)
4. **OEM certification** — "Trontek certified" (quality assurance)
5. **Warranty with clear terms** — Not just "3-year warranty" but "क्या होता है warranty में — replacement या repair, कितने दिन में"
6. **WhatsApp accessibility** — "मेरा WhatsApp number है — कभी भी message कीजिए" (always-on support)
7. **Field team reference** — "हमारी field team ने आपकी shop identify की है" (shows research, not random calling)
